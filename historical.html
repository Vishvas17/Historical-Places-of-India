Historical Places of India

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the southern bank of the Yamuna River in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife Mumtaz Mahal.

Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar is a minaret and "victory tower" that forms part of the Qutb complex, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in the Mehrauli area of Delhi, India. The tower is 73 meters tall and consists of five stories.

Red Fort

The Red Fort is a historic fort in the city of Delhi in India, which served as the main residence of the Mughal Emperors. It was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2007 for its cultural significance.

Hawa Mahal

Hawa Mahal, also known as the "Palace of Winds," is a palace in Jaipur, India. It was built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh and designed by Lal Chand Usta in the form of the crown of Krishna, the Hindu god.

Gateway of India

The Gateway of India is an arch monument built during the 20th century in Bombay, India. It was erected to commemorate the landing of King George V and Queen Mary at Apollo Bunder on their visit to India in 1911.

Atal Bridge (Sabarmati Riverfront, Ahmedabad)

The Atal Bridge on Sabarmati Riverfront in Ahmedabad is an iconic cable-stayed bridge named in honor of former Prime Minister Atal Bihari Vajpayee. The bridge spans the Sabarmati River, connecting the eastern and western banks of the city.

Khajuraho Temple

The Khajuraho Group of Monuments is a group of Hindu and Jain temples in Madhya Pradesh, India. They are known for their stunning erotic sculptures and intricate architecture, dating back to the 10th century.

Victoria Memorial

The Victoria Memorial is a large marble building in Kolkata, West Bengal, India. It was built between 1906 and 1921 in memory of Queen Victoria. The memorial's architecture reflects a blend of British and Mughal styles.

Jallianwala Bagh

Jallianwala Bagh is a public garden in Amritsar, Punjab, India, and is known for the Jallianwala Bagh massacre that took place on April 13, 1919. The site is now a memorial to those who lost their lives in the tragic incident.
